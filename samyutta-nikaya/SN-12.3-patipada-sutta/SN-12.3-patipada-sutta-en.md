# The Discourse on the Path of Practice
## Paṭipadāsutta (SN 12.3)

> Sāvatthiyaṁ viharati …pe…
> 
> “micchāpaṭipadañca vo, bhikkhave, desessāmi sammāpaṭipadañca.
> Taṁ suṇātha, sādhukaṁ manasi karotha, bhāsissāmī”ti.

Staying near Sāvatthī... and so on...

“Monks, I will teach you the wrong path of practice (*micchāpaṭipadā*) and the right path of practice (*sammāpaṭipadā*). Listen, pay close attention, and I will speak.”

> “Evaṁ, bhante”ti kho te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṁ.
> Bhagavā etadavoca:

“Yes, Bhante,” those monks replied to the Blessed One.
The Blessed One said this:

> “Katamā ca, bhikkhave, micchāpaṭipadā?
> Avijjāpaccayā, bhikkhave, saṅkhārā;
> 
> saṅkhārapaccayā viññāṇaṁ …pe…
> evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa samudayo hoti.
> Ayaṁ vuccati, bhikkhave, micchāpaṭipadā.

“And what, monks, is the wrong path of practice?
With ignorance (*avijjā*) as condition, mental conditioning (*saṅkhāra*s) arise;
with mental conditioning as condition, consciousness (*viññāṇa*) arises... and so on...
Thus is the origin of this entire mass of suffering.
This, monks, is called the wrong path of practice.

> Katamā ca, bhikkhave, sammāpaṭipadā?
> Avijjāya tveva asesavirāganirodhā saṅkhāranirodho;
> saṅkhāranirodhā viññāṇanirodho …pe…
> evametassa kevalassa dukkhakkhandhassa nirodho hoti.
> Ayaṁ vuccati, bhikkhave, sammāpaṭipadā”ti.

“And what, monks, is the right path of practice?
But with the complete fading away and cessation of ignorance, mental conditioning ceases;
with the cessation of mental conditioning, consciousness ceases... and so on...
Thus is the cessation of this entire mass of suffering.
This, monks, is called the right path of practice.”

> Tatiyaṁ.

(Third)

## Translator Notes

1. **Intimate Teacher-to-Student Voice**: The English translation maintains a direct and experiential tone, matching Goenkaji's spoken style.
2. **Path of Practice (`paṭipadā`)**: Translated as "path of practice" to capture both the active journey and the pragmatic methodology of Vipassana.
3. **Arises/Ceases in Abbreviated Chain**: Repeated the verbs "arises" and "ceases" for each initial link in the abbreviated dependent origination chain to maintain clarity and cadence.
