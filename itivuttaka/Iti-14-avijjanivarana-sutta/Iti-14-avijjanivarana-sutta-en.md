# The Hindrance of Ignorance
## Avijjānīvaraṇa Sutta (Iti 14)

> Vuttañhetaṁ bhagavatā vuttamarahatāti me sutaṁ:
> 
> “Nāhaṁ, bhikkhave, aññaṁ ekanīvaraṇampi samanupassāmi yena nīvaraṇena nivutā pajā dīgharattaṁ sandhāvanti saṁsaranti yathayidaṁ, bhikkhave, avijjānīvaraṇaṁ.
> Avijjānīvaraṇena hi, bhikkhave, nivutā pajā dīgharattaṁ sandhāvanti saṁsarantī”ti.

This was said by the Blessed One, said by the Arahant, as I have heard:

"Monks, I do not see any other single hindrance by which people are obstructed, running and wandering in saṁsāra for such a long time, like the hindrance of ignorance.
Indeed, obstructed by the hindrance of ignorance, monks, people run and wander in saṁsāra for a long time."

> Etamatthaṁ bhagavā avoca.
> Tatthetaṁ iti vuccati:

The Blessed One said this.
In this connection, this was said:

> “Natthañño ekadhammopi,
> yenevaṁ nivutā pajā;
> Saṁsaranti ahorattaṁ,
> yathā mohena āvutā.

"There is no other single thing
by which people are so obstructed;
wandering day and night,
shrouded as they are by delusion.

> Ye ca mohaṁ pahantvāna,
> tamokhandhaṁ padālayuṁ;
> Na te puna saṁsaranti,
> hetu tesaṁ na vijjatī”ti.

But those who, having abandoned delusion,
shatter this mass of darkness,
will wander no more,
for the cause of their wandering is no longer found."

> Ayampi attho vutto bhagavatā, iti me sutanti.
> 
> Catutthaṁ.

This matter too was spoken by the Blessed One, as I have heard.

(Fourth)

| **avijjā** | avijjā | ignorance | Hindrance of Ignorance | The fundamental lack of understanding of the true nature of reality (impermanence, suffering, and not-self), serving as the root cause of saṁsāra. |

## Translator Notes

1. **Ignorance as a Hindrance (*Nīvaraṇa*)**: In standard terminology, five specific hindrances (sensual desire, ill-will, sloth/torpor, restlessness/worry, doubt) are discussed. Here, the Buddha targets ignorance (*avijjā*) as the ultimate global hindrance that veils humanity from truth.
2. **Delusion (*Moha*)**: Used in the verse parallel to ignorance in the prose. Delusion is the active state of misperception that leads the mind to react blindly with craving or aversion.
3. **Mass of Darkness (*Tamokhandha*)**: A powerful metaphor representing the deep-seated, accumulated layers of mental conditioning (*saṅkhāra*) that Vipassana meditation aims to systematically dissolve and shatter through the light of wisdom (*paññā*).
