# The Simile of the Saw
## Kakacūpama Sutta (MN 21)

> Ubhatodaṇḍakena cepi, bhikkhave, kakacena corā ocarakā aṅgamaṅgāni okanteyyuṁ, tatrāpi yo mano padūseyya, na me so tena sāsanakaro.
> Tatrāpi vo, bhikkhave, evaṁ sikkhitabbaṁ:
> ‘na ceva no cittaṁ vipariṇataṁ bhavissati, na ca pāpikaṁ vācaṁ nicchāressāma, hitānukampī ca viharissāma mettacittā na dosantarā.
> Tañca puggalaṁ mettāsahagatena cetasā pharitvā viharissāma tadārammaṇañca sabbāvantaṁ lokaṁ mettāsahagatena cetasā vipulena mahaggatena appamāṇena averena abyābajjhena pharitvā viharissāmā’ti.
> Evañhi vo, bhikkhave, sikkhitabbaṁ.

"Monks, even if bandits were to cut you limb from limb with a two-handled saw, anyone who harbors a hateful mind in that moment is not following my teaching.
Even then, monks, this is how you must train yourselves:
'Our minds will remain unaffected, and we will not utter any harsh words. We will dwell with compassion and goodwill, with a mind full of loving-kindness (*mettā*) and free of any hatred. We will radiate *mettā* to that person, and starting from them, we will fill the entire world with *mettā*—with a mind that is vast, exalted, boundless, free from enmity, and free from ill-will.'
Indeed, monks, this is how you should train yourselves."

> Imañca tumhe, bhikkhave, kakacūpamaṁ ovādaṁ abhikkhaṇaṁ manasi kareyyātha. Variant: Imañca → imañce (?)
> Passatha no tumhe, bhikkhave, taṁ vacanapathaṁ, aṇuṁ vā thūlaṁ vā, yaṁ tumhe nādhivāseyyāthā”ti?

"Monks, you should constantly reflect on this advice of the simile of the saw (*kakacūpama*).
Do you see any kind of speech, subtle or coarse, that you would not be able to endure?"

> “No hetaṁ, bhante”.

"No, Venerable Sir."

> “Tasmātiha, bhikkhave, imaṁ kakacūpamaṁ ovādaṁ abhikkhaṇaṁ manasikarotha.
> Taṁ vo bhavissati dīgharattaṁ hitāya sukhāyā”ti.

"Therefore, monks, keep this advice of the *kakacūpama* constantly in mind. That will bring you long-lasting welfare and happiness (*sukha*)."

> Idamavoca bhagavā.
> Attamanā te bhikkhū bhagavato bhāsitaṁ abhinandunti.

This is what the Blessed One said. The monks were delighted and rejoiced in the Blessed One's words.

## Translator Notes

1. **Intimate and Direct Register**: Kept the language simple, direct, and conversational to preserve the warm "teacher-to-student" voice of Goenkaji's discourse style rather than standard scholarly/academic translation prose.
2. **First-Occurrence Pāli Integration**: Incorporated the Pāli terms *mettā*, *kakacūpama*, and *sukha* inline with their respective glosses on their first occurrence, ensuring that the Pāli vocabulary remains present and clear for the practitioner's understanding.
3. **Lineage Terminology**: Used standard lineage-faithful English terms (e.g., "loving-kindness", "goodwill", "Venerable Sir", "welfare and happiness") matching the terminology found in Goenka-tradition publications.
