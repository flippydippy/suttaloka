# A Heap of Bones
## Aṭṭhipuñjasutta (Iti 24)

> Vuttañhetaṁ bhagavatā vuttamarahatāti me sutaṁ:

This was said by the Blessed One, said by the Arahant, as I have heard:

> “Ekapuggalassa, bhikkhave, kappaṁ sandhāvato saṁsarato siyā evaṁ mahā aṭṭhikaṅkalo aṭṭhipuñjo aṭṭhirāsi yathāyaṁ vepullo pabbato, sace saṁhārako assa, sambhatañca na vinasseyyā”ti.

"Monks, if a single person were to wander and transmigrate on for an eon, he would leave behind a chain of bones, a pile of bones, a heap of bones, as large as this Mount Vepulla, if there were someone to collect them and the collection were not destroyed."

> Etamatthaṁ bhagavā avoca.
> Tatthetaṁ iti vuccati:

The Blessed One said this.
In this connection, this was said:

> “Ekassekena kappena,
> puggalassaṭṭhisañcayo;
> Siyā pabbatasamo rāsi,
> iti vuttaṁ mahesinā.

"The accumulation of a single person's bones
accumulated over the course of a single eon
would be a heap equal to a mountain:
so said the Great Seer.

> So kho panāyaṁ akkhāto,
> vepullo pabbato mahā;
> Uttaro gijjhakūṭassa,
> magadhānaṁ giribbaje.

That great mountain is declared to be
Mount Vepulla, north of Vulture’s Peak,
in the mountain ring of Magadha.

> Yato ca ariyasaccāni,
> sammappaññāya passati;
> Dukkhaṁ dukkhasamuppādaṁ,
> dukkhassa ca atikkamaṁ;
> Ariyañcaṭṭhaṅgikaṁ maggaṁ,
> dukkhūpasamagāminaṁ.

But when one sees the Noble Truths
with right wisdom—
suffering, the arising of suffering,
the overcoming of suffering,
and the Noble Eightfold Path
leading to the calming of suffering—

> Sa sattakkhattuṁ paramaṁ,
> sandhāvitvāna puggalo;
> Dukkhassantakaro hoti,
> sabbasaṁyojanakkhayā”ti.

Having wandered on at most seven times more,
that person makes an end of suffering
by destroying all fetters."

> Ayampi attho vutto bhagavatā, iti me sutanti.
> 
> Catutthaṁ.

This matter too was spoken by the Blessed One, as I have heard.

(Fourth)

## Translator Notes

1. **The Magnitude of Saṃsāra**: The simile of the mountain of bones is one of the most vivid illustrations of the beginningless nature of *saṃsāra*. It is intended to evoke a sense of urgency (*saṁvega*) in the practitioner to work diligently toward liberation rather than wasting lifetimes in reaction to transient pleasures.
2. **Mount Vepulla**: Located near Rājagaha (the capital of Magadha), Mount Vepulla was a prominent local landmark. The Buddha uses this physical mountain, visible to his audience, to make the abstract duration of an eon (*kappa*) visually and emotionally tangible.
3. **The Limit of Seven Rebirths**: The verse refers to the "stream-enterer" (*sotāpanna*), a noble disciple (*ariya*) who has gained the first glimpse of *nibbāna*. Through the destruction of the first three fetters (*saṃyojana*), they are guaranteed to attain full liberation within at most seven more lifetimes.
