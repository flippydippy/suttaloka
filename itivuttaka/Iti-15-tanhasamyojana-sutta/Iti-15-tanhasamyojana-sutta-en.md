# The Fetter of Craving
## Taṇhāsaṁyojana Sutta (Iti 15)

> Vuttañhetaṁ bhagavatā vuttamarahatāti me sutaṁ:
> 
> “Nāhaṁ, bhikkhave, aññaṁ ekasaṁyojanampi samanupassāmi yena saṁyojanena saṁyuttā sattā dīgharattaṁ sandhāvanti saṁsaranti yathayidaṁ, bhikkhave, taṇhāsaṁyojanaṁ.
> Taṇhāsaṁyojanena hi, bhikkhave, saṁyuttā sattā dīgharattaṁ sandhāvanti saṁsarantī”ti.

This was said by the Blessed One, said by the Arahant, as I have heard:

"Monks, I do not see any other single fetter by which beings are bound, running and wandering in saṁsāra for such a long time, like the fetter of craving.
Indeed, bound by the fetter of craving, monks, beings run and wander in saṁsāra for a long time."

> Etamatthaṁ bhagavā avoca.
> Tatthetaṁ iti vuccati:

The Blessed One said this.
In this connection, this was said:

> “Taṇhādutiyo puriso,
> dīghamaddhāna saṁsaraṁ;
> Itthabhāvaññathābhāvaṁ,
> saṁsāraṁ nātivattati.

"A person with craving as their companion,
wanders for a long time in saṁsāra;
they do not transcend the cycle of becoming—
from this state to another.

> Etamādīnavaṁ ñatvā,
> Taṇhaṁ dukkhassa sambhavaṁ;
> Vītataṇho anādāno,
> Sato bhikkhu paribbaje”ti.

Knowing this danger,
that craving is the origin of suffering,
free from craving, without clinging,
a bhikkhu should wander mindfully."

> Ayampi attho vutto bhagavatā, iti me sutanti.
> 
> Pañcamaṁ.

This matter too was spoken by the Blessed One, as I have heard.

(Fifth)

## Translator Notes

1. **Opening Formula (*Vuttañhetaṁ...*)**: This is the standard opening signature of the "Thus Said" collection (*Itivuttaka*), emphasizing that the following text was directly spoken by the Buddha and verified by the Arahants.
2. **Craving as the Companion (*Taṇhādutiyo*)**: The term *taṇhādutiyo* literally means "having craving as one's second" or "companion." As long as craving accompanies the mind, one cannot escape the cycle of samsara.
3. **The Fetter (*Saṁyojana*)**: While there are ten fetters in Buddhist doctrine, this sutta highlights craving as the single most critical and binding fetter that drives the wheel of suffering.
