# Taṇhā Sutta
## Taṇhāsutta (AN 10.62)

> 7. Yamakavagga
> Taṇhāsutta
> “Purimā, bhikkhave, koṭi na paññāyati bhavataṇhāya: ‘ito pubbe bhavataṇhā nāhosi, atha pacchā samabhavī’ti.
> Evañcetaṁ, bhikkhave, vuccati, atha ca pana paññāyati: ‘idappaccayā bhavataṇhā’ti.

7. Pairs
The Discourse on Craving
“Monks, a first beginning of craving for existence (*bhavataṇhā*) cannot be known, such that one could say: ‘Before this, craving for existence did not exist; then it came into being.’ But even though this is said, monks, it is still known: ‘Craving for existence is conditioned by this.’

> Bhavataṇhampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ. Variant: Bhavataṇhampāhaṁ → bhavataṇhāmpāhaṁ (csp1ed, csp2ed)
> Ko cāhāro bhavataṇhāya?
> ‘Avijjā’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Craving for existence, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for craving for existence? ‘Ignorance (*avijjā*)’ should be the answer.

> Avijjampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro avijjāya?
> ‘Pañca nīvaraṇā’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Ignorance, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for ignorance? ‘The five hindrances (*nīvaraṇa*)’ should be the answer.

> Pañca nīvaraṇepāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāre vadāmi, no anāhāre.
> Ko cāhāro pañcannaṁ nīvaraṇānaṁ?
> ‘Tīṇi duccaritānī’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“The five hindrances, monks, I say have a nutriment; they are not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for the five hindrances? ‘The three kinds of wrong conduct (*duccarita*)’ should be the answer.

> Tīṇipāhaṁ, bhikkhave, duccaritāni sāhārāni vadāmi, no anāhārāni.
> Ko cāhāro tiṇṇannaṁ duccaritānaṁ?
> ‘Indriyaasaṁvaro’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“The three kinds of wrong conduct, monks, I say have a nutriment; they are not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for the three kinds of wrong conduct? ‘Lack of sense restraint (*indriyaasaṃvara*)’ should be the answer.

> Indriyaasaṁvarampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro indriyaasaṁvarassa?
> ‘Asatāsampajaññan’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Lack of sense restraint, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for lack of sense restraint? ‘Lack of awareness and constant thorough understanding (*asatāsampajañña*)’ should be the answer.

> Asatāsampajaññampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro asatā sampajaññassa?
> ‘Ayonisomanasikāro’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Lack of awareness and constant thorough understanding, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for lack of awareness and constant thorough understanding? ‘Improper attention (*ayonisomanasikāra*)’ should be the answer.

> Ayonisomanasikārampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro ayonisomanasikārassa?
> ‘Assaddhiyan’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Improper attention, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for improper attention? ‘Lack of trust (*assaddhiya*)’ should be the answer.

> Assaddhiyampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro assaddhiyassa?
> ‘Asaddhammassavanan’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Lack of trust, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for lack of trust? ‘Hearing the untrue teaching (*asaddhammassavana*)’ should be the answer.

> Assaddhammassavanampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro asaddhammassavanassa?
> ‘Asappurisasaṁsevo’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Hearing the untrue teaching, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for hearing the untrue teaching? ‘Associating with unwise people (*asappurisasaṃseva*)’ should be the answer.

> Iti kho, bhikkhave, asappurisasaṁsevo paripūro assaddhammassavanaṁ paripūreti, asaddhammassavanaṁ paripūraṁ assaddhiyaṁ paripūreti, assaddhiyaṁ paripūraṁ ayonisomanasikāraṁ paripūreti, ayonisomanasikāro paripūro asatāsampajaññaṁ paripūreti, asatāsampajaññaṁ paripūraṁ indriyaasaṁvaraṁ paripūreti, indriyaasaṁvaro paripūro tīṇi duccaritāni paripūreti, tīṇi duccaritāni paripūrāni pañca nīvaraṇe paripūrenti, pañca nīvaraṇā paripūrā avijjaṁ paripūrenti, avijjā paripūrā bhavataṇhaṁ paripūreti;
> evametissā bhavataṇhāya āhāro hoti, evañca pāripūri.

“Thus, monks, when association with unwise people is complete, it fulfills hearing the untrue teaching; hearing the untrue teaching, when complete, fulfills lack of trust; lack of trust, when complete, fulfills improper attention; improper attention, when complete, fulfills lack of awareness and constant thorough understanding; lack of awareness and constant thorough understanding, when complete, fulfills lack of sense restraint; lack of sense restraint, when complete, fulfills the three kinds of wrong conduct; the three kinds of wrong conduct, when complete, fulfill the five hindrances; the five hindrances, when complete, fulfill ignorance; ignorance, when complete, fulfills craving for existence. Such is the nutriment of this craving for existence, and such is its fulfillment.

> Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, uparipabbate thullaphusitake deve vassante taṁ udakaṁ yathāninnaṁ pavattamānaṁ pabbatakandarapadarasākhā paripūreti, pabbatakandarapadarasākhā paripūrā kusobbhe paripūrenti, kusobbhā paripūrā mahāsobbhe paripūrenti, mahāsobbhā paripūrā kunnadiyo paripūrenti, kunnadiyo paripūrā mahānadiyo paripūrenti, mahānadiyo paripūrā mahāsamuddaṁ sāgaraṁ paripūrenti;
> evametassa mahāsamuddassa sāgarassa āhāro hoti, evañca pāripūri.

“Just as when rain falls in fat drops on the mountain tops, the water, flowing downhill, fills the clefts, crevices, and gullies of the mountain; when the clefts, crevices, and gullies of the mountain are full, they fill the small pools; when the small pools are full, they fill the large pools; when the large pools are full, they fill the small streams; when the small streams are full, they fill the great rivers; when the great rivers are full, they fill the great ocean. Such is the nutriment of the great ocean, and such is its fulfillment.

> Evamevaṁ kho, bhikkhave, asappurisasaṁsevo paripūro assaddhammassavanaṁ paripūreti, asaddhammassavanaṁ paripūraṁ assaddhiyaṁ paripūreti, assaddhiyaṁ paripūraṁ ayonisomanasikāraṁ paripūreti, ayonisomanasikāro paripūro asatāsampajaññaṁ paripūreti, asatāsampajaññaṁ paripūraṁ indriyaasaṁvaraṁ paripūreti, indriyaasaṁvaro paripūro tīṇi duccaritāni paripūreti, tīṇi duccaritāni paripūrāni pañca nīvaraṇe paripūrenti, pañca nīvaraṇā paripūrā avijjaṁ paripūrenti, avijjā paripūrā bhavataṇhaṁ paripūreti;
> evametissā bhavataṇhāya āhāro hoti, evañca pāripūri.

“In the same way, monks, when association with unwise people is complete, it fulfills hearing the untrue teaching; hearing the untrue teaching, when complete, fulfills lack of trust; lack of trust, when complete, fulfills improper attention; improper attention, when complete, fulfills lack of awareness and constant thorough understanding; lack of awareness and constant thorough understanding, when complete, fulfills lack of sense restraint; lack of sense restraint, when complete, fulfills the three kinds of wrong conduct; the three kinds of wrong conduct, when complete, fulfill the five hindrances; the five hindrances, when complete, fulfill ignorance; ignorance, when complete, fulfills craving for existence. Such is the nutriment of this craving for existence, and such is its fulfillment.

> Vijjāvimuttimpāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro vijjāvimuttiyā?
> ‘Satta bojjhaṅgā’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“True knowledge and liberation (*vijjāvimutti*), monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for true knowledge and liberation? ‘The seven factors of awakening (*bojjhaṅga*)’ should be the answer.

> Sattapāhaṁ, bhikkhave, bojjhaṅge sāhāre vadāmi, no anāhāre.
> Ko cāhāro sattannaṁ bojjhaṅgānaṁ?
> ‘Cattāro satipaṭṭhānā’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“The seven factors of awakening, monks, I say have a nutriment; they are not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for the seven factors of awakening? ‘The four establishments of mindfulness (*satipaṭṭhāna*)’ should be the answer.

> Cattāropāhaṁ, bhikkhave, satipaṭṭhāne sāhāre vadāmi, no anāhāre.
> Ko cāhāro catunnaṁ satipaṭṭhānānaṁ?
> ‘Tīṇi sucaritānī’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“The four establishments of mindfulness, monks, I say have a nutriment; they are not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for the four establishments of mindfulness? ‘The three kinds of good conduct (*sucarita*)’ should be the answer.

> Tīṇipāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sucaritāni sāhārāni vadāmi, no anāhārāni.
> Ko cāhāro tiṇannaṁ sucaritānaṁ?
> ‘Indriyasaṁvaro’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“The three kinds of good conduct, monks, I say have a nutriment; they are not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for the three kinds of good conduct? ‘Sense restraint (*indriyasaṃvara*)’ should be the answer.

> Indriyasaṁvarampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro indriyasaṁvarassa?
> ‘Satisampajaññan’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Sense restraint, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for sense restraint? ‘Awareness and constant thorough understanding (*satisampajañña*)’ should be the answer.

> Satisampajaññampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro satisampajaññassa?
> ‘Yonisomanasikāro’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Awareness and constant thorough understanding, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for awareness and constant thorough understanding? ‘Proper attention (*yonisomanasikāra*)’ should be the answer.

> Yonisomanasikārampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro yonisomanasikārassa?
> ‘Saddhā’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Proper attention, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for proper attention? ‘Trust (*saddhā*)’ should be the answer.

> Saddhampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro saddhāya?
> ‘Saddhammassavanan’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Trust, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for trust? ‘Hearing the true teaching (*saddhammassavana*)’ should be the answer.

> Saddhammassavanampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, sāhāraṁ vadāmi, no anāhāraṁ.
> Ko cāhāro saddhammassavanassa?
> ‘Sappurisasaṁsevo’tissa vacanīyaṁ.

“Hearing the true teaching, monks, I say has a nutriment; it is not without nutriment. And what is the nutriment for hearing the true teaching? ‘Associating with virtuous people (*sappurisasaṃseva*)’ should be the answer.

> Iti kho, bhikkhave, sappurisasaṁsevo paripūro saddhammassavanaṁ paripūreti, saddhammassavanaṁ paripūraṁ saddhaṁ paripūreti, saddhā paripūrā yonisomanasikāraṁ paripūreti, yonisomanasikāro paripro satisampajaññaṁ paripūreti, satisampajaññaṁ paripūraṁ indriyasaṁvaraṁ paripūreti, indriyasaṁvaro paripūro tīṇi sucaritāni paripūreti, tīṇi sucaritāni paripūrāni cattāro satipaṭṭhāne paripūrenti, cattāro satipaṭṭhānā paripūrā satta bojjhaṅge paripūrenti, satta bojjhaṅgā paripūrā vijjāvimuttiṁ paripūrenti;
> evametissā vijjāvimuttiyā āhāro hoti, evañca pāripūri.

“Thus, monks, when association with virtuous people is complete, it fulfills hearing the true teaching; hearing the true teaching, when complete, fulfills trust; trust, when complete, fulfills proper attention; proper attention, when complete, fulfills awareness and constant thorough understanding; awareness and constant thorough understanding, when complete, fulfills sense restraint; sense restraint, when complete, fulfills the three kinds of good conduct; the three kinds of good conduct, when complete, fulfill the four establishments of mindfulness; the four establishments of mindfulness, when complete, fulfill the seven factors of awakening; the seven factors of awakening, when complete, fulfill true knowledge and liberation. Such is the nutriment of this true knowledge and liberation, and such is its fulfillment.

> Seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, uparipabbate thullaphusitake deve vassante taṁ udakaṁ yathāninnaṁ pavattamānaṁ …pe…
> evametassa mahāsamuddassa sāgarassa āhāro hoti, evañca pāripūri.

“Just as when rain falls in fat drops on the mountain tops, the water, flowing downhill ... [and so on] ... fills the great ocean. Such is the nutriment of the great ocean, and such is its fulfillment.

> Evamevaṁ kho, bhikkhave, sappurisasaṁsevo paripūro saddhammassavanaṁ paripūreti …pe…
> evametissā vijjāvimuttiyā āhāro hoti, evañca pāripūrī”ti.

“In the same way, monks, when association with virtuous people is complete, it fulfills hearing the true teaching ... [and so on] ... fulfills true knowledge and liberation. Such is the nutriment of this true knowledge and liberation, and such is its fulfillment.”

> Dutiyaṁ.

The second.
