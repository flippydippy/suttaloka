# First Dabba Discourse
## Paṭhamadabbasutta (Ud 8.9)

> Evaṁ me sutaṁ—
> ekaṁ samayaṁ bhagavā rājagahe viharati veḷuvane kalandakanivāpe.
> Atha kho āyasmā dabbo mallaputto yena bhagavā tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā bhagavantaṁ abhivādetvā ekamantaṁ nisīdi.
> Ekamantaṁ nisinno kho āyasmā dabbo mallaputto bhagavantaṁ etadavoca:
> “parinibbānakālo me dāni, sugatā”ti.
> “Yassadāni tvaṁ, dabba, kālaṁ maññasī”ti.

Thus have I heard—
At one time the Blessed One was staying at Rājagaha in the Bamboo Grove, the Squirrels' Feeding Ground.
Then the Venerable Dabba Mallaputta went to the Blessed One, paid respects, and sat down to one side.
Sitting there to one side, the Venerable Dabba Mallaputta said to the Blessed One:
“Now is the time for my final passing away (*parinibbāna*), O Happy One (*sugata*).”
“Dabba, do what you think is best now.”

> Atha kho āyasmā dabbo mallaputto uṭṭhāyāsanā bhagavantaṁ abhivādetvā padakkhiṇaṁ katvā vehāsaṁ abbhuggantvā ākāse antalikkhe pallaṅkena nisīditvā tejodhātuṁ samāpajjitvā vuṭṭhahitvā parinibbāyi.

Then the Venerable Dabba Mallaputta rose from his seat, paid respects to the Blessed One, circled him keeping him on his right, and rose up into the air. Sitting cross-legged in the sky, he entered the fire element (*tejodhātu*), emerged from it, and attained *parinibbāna*.

> Atha kho āyasmato dabbassa mallaputtassa vehāsaṁ abbhuggantvā ākāse antalikkhe pallaṅkena nisīditvā tejodhātuṁ samāpajjitvā vuṭṭhahitvā parinibbutassa sarīrassa jhāyamānassa ḍayhamānassa neva chārikā paññāyittha na masi.
> Seyyathāpi nāma sappissa vā telassa vā jhāyamānassa ḍayhamānassa neva chārikā paññāyati na masi;
> evamevaṁ āyasmato dabbassa mallaputtassa vehāsaṁ abbhuggantvā ākāse antalikkhe pallaṅkena nisīditvā tejodhātuṁ samāpajjitvā vuṭṭhahitvā parinibbutassa sarīrassa jhāyamānassa ḍayhamānassa neva chārikā paññāyittha na masīti.

And when the body of the Venerable Dabba Mallaputta—who had risen into the air, sat cross-legged in the sky, entered the fire element, emerged from it, and attained *parinibbāna*—was burned and consumed, neither ashes nor soot remained.
Just as when ghee or oil is burned and consumed, neither ashes nor soot remain; in the very same way, when the body of the Venerable Dabba Mallaputta—who had risen into the air, sat cross-legged in the sky, entered the fire element, emerged from it, and attained *parinibbāna*—was burned and consumed, neither ashes nor soot remained.

> Atha kho bhagavā etamatthaṁ viditvā tāyaṁ velāyaṁ imaṁ udānaṁ udānesi:

Then the Blessed One, understanding this, uttered this inspired utterance on that occasion:

> “Abhedi kāyo nirodhi saññā,
> Vedanā sītibhaviṁsu sabbā;
> Vūpasamiṁsu saṅkhārā,
> Viññāṇaṁ atthamāgamā”ti.

“The body broke up, perception (*saññā*) ceased,
All sensations (*vedanā*s) cooled down;
All mental conditioning (*saṅkhāra*s) subsided,
Consciousness (*viññāṇa*) came to an end.”

> Navamaṁ.

(Ninth)

## Translator Notes

1. **Intimate Teacher-to-Student Voice**: The English translation maintains a direct and experiential tone, matching Goenkaji's spoken style.
2. **Burned and Consumed (`jhāyamānassa ḍayhamānassa`)**: Translated as "burned and consumed" for both the body of Dabba Mallaputta and the ghee/oil in the simile, maintaining parallelism and avoiding technical words like "cremated" that do not apply to liquid fats.
3. **Fire Element (`tejodhātu`)**: Retained in Pāli with an English gloss to emphasize the meditative attainment of fire-element concentration (*tejodhātu-samāpatti*) which led to the residue-free passing away.
4. **The Aggregates in Verse**: Restructured the verse to clearly isolate the four mental aggregates (*saññā*, *vedanā*, *saṅkhāra*, *viññāṇa*) and their cessation, mirroring the meditative path of observing the arising and passing of sensations.
