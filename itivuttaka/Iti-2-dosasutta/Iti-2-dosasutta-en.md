# Abandoning Anger
## Dosasutta (Iti 2)

> Vuttañhetaṁ bhagavatā vuttamarahatāti me sutaṁ:
> 
> “Ekadhammaṁ, bhikkhave, pajahatha;
> ahaṁ vo pāṭibhogo anāgāmitāya.
> Katamaṁ ekadhammaṁ?
> Dosaṁ, bhikkhave, ekadhammaṁ pajahatha;
> ahaṁ vo pāṭibhogo anāgāmitāyā”ti.

This was said by the Blessed One, said by the Arahant, as I have heard:

"Monks, abandon one thing;
I guarantee you the state of non-returning.
Which one thing?
Monks, abandon anger;
I guarantee you the state of non-returning."

> Etamatthaṁ bhagavā avoca.
> Tatthetaṁ iti vuccati:

The Blessed One said this.
In this connection, this was said:

> “Yena dosena duṭṭhāse,
> sattā gacchanti duggatiṁ;
> Taṁ dosaṁ sammadaññāya,
> pajahanti vipassino;
> Pahāya na punāyanti,
> imaṁ lokaṁ kudācanan”ti.

"Defiled by which anger,
beings go to a state of misery;
fully understanding that anger,
those with insight abandon it.
Having abandoned it, they never return
to this world again."

> Ayampi attho vutto bhagavatā, iti me sutanti.
> 
> Dutiyaṁ.

This matter too was spoken by the Blessed One, as I have heard.

(Second)

## Translator Notes

1. **Aversion as Anger (*Dosa*)**: While *dosa* is often translated as anger, in the Vipassana tradition it represents the entire spectrum of aversion—from mild dislike and irritation to intense hatred and anger—arising in response to unpleasant sensations.
2. **Guarantor (*Pāṭibhoga*)**: The Buddha guarantees (*pāṭibhoga*) the state of non-returning (*anāgāmitā*) to anyone who completely eradicates anger, as *dosa* is one of the lower fetters (*saṁyojana*) that binds a being to the sensual realm.
3. **Seeing with Insight (*Vipassino*)**: The verse highlights that only those who see clearly (*vipassino*, Vipassana meditators) can fully understand the nature of anger and abandon it. Insight is developed by observing the physical sensations associated with anger without reacting.
